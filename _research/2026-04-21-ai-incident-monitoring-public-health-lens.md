This paper develops a public-health lens for AI incident monitoring, arguing that incident databases alone cannot reveal risk without better information on system prevalence and reporting practices.

It introduces a six-phase framework for incident emergence and uses autonomous-vehicle and deepfake case studies to show how expert judgment, incident data, and statistical tools can support more decision-useful oversight.
